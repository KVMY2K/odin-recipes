Beef Stew

Stew for the cold season!

The best way to prepare beef meat is to make a nice stew out of it. And it's totally customizable too! You may add any vegetables of your liking, or substitute any ingredient with something else. Need a vegan option? Swap the meat for tofu. Don't like carrots? Add sweet potatoes instead. This is how I make it, but you are free to customize it however you see fit.

Ingredients

  • 1kg beef shank
  • 400g tomatoes
  • 0.5kg potatoes
  • 3 large carrots
  • 1 small celery
  • 2 large onions
  • 1/2 head garlic
  • 1 bell pepper of each color
  • olive oil
  • 1l beef stock
  • 1 bottle of red wine
  • 100 ml cream
  • sour cream
  • bayleafs
  • pepper and paprika
  • sourdough bread

Steps

  1. Cut in the meat slightly on the sides to avoid it curling up
  2. Cut the potatoes to roughly equal sized, large cubes, and soak them
  3. Cut one carrot, the celery, onions, and garlic to as small cubes as you can
  4. Cut the remaining carrots to larger cubes, akin to the potatoes
  5. Cut up the bell peppers roughly, around the same size as the carrots
  6. Have a glass of wine
  7. Sear the meat on both sizes over olive oil in a large enough pot for your stew
  8. Remove the seared meat and add a glass of wine to make a demiglaze
  9. Stir it until the bottom is smooth, and remove the demiglaze
  10. Cook the fine veggies in olive oil
  11. Add in the tomatoes when the veggies are ready
  12. When it's reducing, add in your spices and put back the meat and the demiglaze
  13. Add in half the stock, turn the heat down to low, put on a lid
  14. Simmer for 2-3 hours, gradually replacing the reducing stock
  15. Before the last stock exchange (haha!), add in the roughly chopped veggies and cream
  16. Cook until veggies are done
  17. Plate it, top it with olive oil, sour cream, and serve it with sourdough
  18. Don't forget the wine!
